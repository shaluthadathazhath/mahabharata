⚔️ Why is Kurukshetra called Dharmakshetra?

The very first verse of the Bhagavad Gita begins: "Dharmakshetre Kurukshetre" — "In the field of dharma, in Kurukshetra." But why is a battlefield called a field of dharma (righteousness)?

🌿
Sacred since ancient times: Kurukshetra was already considered the most sacred land in Bharatavarsha — the Vedas and Puranas describe it as "the field where the gods themselves perform sacrifices." Long before the war, it was a tirtha (pilgrimage site).
⚖️
The war was fought for Dharma: This was not a war of conquest or greed — it was a war to restore righteousness. The Pandavas fought because their rightful kingdom was stolen and Draupadi was humiliated. Fighting for justice IS dharma.
🕉️
The soil purifies: Ancient tradition holds that dying at Kurukshetra grants liberation (moksha), because the land itself is saturated with dharma. Warriors on both sides who died there attained heaven.
📖
The Gita was spoken here: The fact that the Bhagavad Gita — the highest dharmic teaching — was delivered at Kurukshetra before a single arrow was shot, confirms its status as the field of dharma.
👁️
Krishna's own words: In calling it Dharmakshetra, Krishna himself sanctified the battlefield. His presence transformed a field of war into a field of spiritual revelation.

Today, Kurukshetra in Haryana (160 km north of Delhi) remains one of India's holiest pilgrimage sites. The Brahma Sarovar tank and Jyotisar temple (where the Gita was spoken) attract millions of visitors each year.

Bhagavad Gita

700 verses of timeless wisdom — all 18 chapters with key verses and recitation guide

On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, when Arjuna was overwhelmed with grief and refused to fight, Lord Krishna imparted the divine wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita. This sacred dialogue spans 18 chapters and 700 verses and covers the essence of life, duty, devotion, and liberation.

🎵 How to Recite: Each verse (shloka) follows the anushtubh meter — 8 syllables per quarter, 4 quarters per verse. Recite slowly, pause at the midpoint of each line, and feel the rhythm. Try the audio pronunciation guide below each verse.
