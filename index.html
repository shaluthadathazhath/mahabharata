Understanding the origins of the world's longest epic

The Divine Origins

The Mahabharata was composed by the Sage Vyasa, who witnessed these events. It is said that Lord Ganesha acted as the scribe, writing down the verses as Vyasa dictated them without pause.

Time and Place

The story is set roughly 5,000 years ago (approx. 3100 BCE) in Aryavarta (Northern India). The events center around the Kuru Kingdom, with its capital at Hastinapura and the final battle at Kurukshetra.

Authentic Source

This digital guide primarily follows the Critical Edition published by the Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute (BORI), reconstructed from over 1,200 ancient manuscripts.

Key Terms to Know

Vedas: Ancient books of universal knowledge.
Upanishads: Philosophical teachings about the soul.
Shlokas: The poetic couplets (verses) used to write the epic.
Parvas: The 18 major "Books" or chapters of the story.

Bhagavad Gita

The Song of God — 700 verses of timeless wisdom spoken by Krishna to Arjuna

On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, when Arjuna was overwhelmed with grief and refused to fight, Lord Krishna imparted the divine wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita. This sacred dialogue spans 18 chapters and covers the essence of life, duty, devotion, and liberation.
